WHAT CAN YOU DO WITH CLOUD COMPUTING?

Cloud computing is extremely useful for both commercial and personal use. It provides a safe and secure place in which people may store their files, images and other important data and easily be able to access it via a phone, computer or other electronic device. Cloud computing is used by some of the biggest companies such as Netflix, Xerox, Pinterest, Instagram, Facebook, Apple, Google, and Microsoft. Many companies use cloud computing as a way of making business more efficient and easier to manage by allowing cloud sharing. The cloud is a great way to store data safely without having to purchase an external hard drive or other storage device, and it's affordable and easy to purchase more storage.

Companies such as Apple and Google are both large supporters, and providers, of cloud computing.
Apple's iCloud comes with 5GB of storage with each account. You can easily upgrade your storage capacity, with 50GB coming in at $0.99/month, 200GB at $2.99/month, and 2TB at $9.99/month.
Google has their Drive. Google Drive comes with access to many of their services, such as Docs, Sheets, Slides, Forms, and more. Drive is easy to use and access, and a great way for small companies and schools to allow students and employees to have accounts and easily be able to store data and share that data with others. Google offers 15GB free, and you can upgrade to 100GB for $1.99/month or $19.99/year. 1TB is also an option, coming in at $9.99/month or $99.99/year.

Schools can really make great use of cloud computing services. Google's offering seems to be the most appealing for schools. Drive makes it very easy to save work. If you don't finish in class, don't worry! You can just log in to your account at home and finish there. Google Classroom gives teachers all of the tools they need to assign and grade assignments. Teachers can post something like "Write a four-sentence summary over this selection," and a file will be attatched with the passage. Students can then click the create button and make a quick Google Doc with their summary. In the top right of the Google Doc toolbar, there will be a button that says "Turn in" that students can use to turn in their assignment once they're done. Students also receive a school email that they can use for school purposes. If students have questions about their homework, they can send a quick email to a teacher and get a response. For group projects, Google's suite of apps such as Docs, Sheets, Slides, etc. allows real-time collaboration between multiple people. That way, you can get more done at once.
