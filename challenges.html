WHAT ARE THE CHALLENGES PEOPLE ARE FACING WITH CLOUD COMPUTING?

There are quite a few challenges in regards to cloud computing. Namely, security and privacy. With the convenience of being able to access files from anywhere comes a price. Your files will be uploaded to the internet, which can — and has been — hacked. Your data is not 100% safe while uploaded to the cloud, despite many companies' best efforts. Your data may even be given away by some companies. Thinking otherwise is somewhat naïve, and that naïveté could have serious consequences.

Reliability is also an issue. If, and when, these services get hacked, you might not only have your privacy taken away, but your files too. Having your files somewhere besides your computer can be an advantage, but it's also a major weakness for cloud computing. More and more businesses and schools are starting to rely upon the cloud. If the service that's being used goes down, you may not be able to complete what you need to. Files in the cloud can become inaccessible. At a workplace, you may not be able to fulfill a client's order and lose money. At school, you won't be able to finish an assignment and might get a bad grade, depending on how mean or nice your teacher is. Internet can also go down. That can be an issue as well, seeing as you need internet to access cloud computing services.

Don't forget about availability, either. Schools and businesses can be set up in places where the average person may not have fast enough internet, or internet at all, to take advantage of the cloud. Sure, they may be able to use it at the school or business, but then it becomes a question of if it's worth it or not. If people are only using it at one place, what's the purpose of using the cloud instead of just saving it to the computer's hard drive or a flash drive? An organization must consider whether it's more cost effective to purchase a cloud computing plan or just to give everyone a flash drive.
