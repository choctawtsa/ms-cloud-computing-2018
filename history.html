HOW DID CLOUD COMPUTING COME ABOUT?

Cloud computing, as we know it today, took quite a while to get to where it is. Its inception was quite a while ago, when Myspace was called part of the "cloud" and Amazon was only known for selling books and movies.

From 2000 through 2005, the dot-com bubble burst lead to the introduction of cloud computing. Failing internet companies dragged on the tech industry. Any internet companies that survived modernized their IT architectures, making them more robust and finding solutions that were more efficient.
Amazon entered the cloud market in 2006. They were one of the few internet companies that survived the dot-com bubble burst, but they decided that they would "pioneer the cloud computing movement." Amazon introduced their Amazon Web Services, or AWS for short. AWS is still being used today, powering many businesses beyond Amazon. Two years later, in 2008, Google, Cisco Systems, Myspace, and Microsoft join Amazon in offering cloud services.

2009 through 2012 was a great time for the cloud. It picked up acceptance in small and enterprise businesses. AWS picked up the Obama for America team and had a partnership with NASA for the Curiosity Rover landing on Mars in 2012. Netflix joined Amazon Web Services in 2009. Apple joined the cloud computing business in 2011, introducing iCloud for its customers at its annual WWDC event.

Today, most people seem to be using cloud computing, whether it's at work, school, or home.
